Introduction

I am an electrical designer with experience in a variety of electrical applications from commercial to utility/industrial. I also have some engineering experience and am gearing my career more towards the engineering aspect of electrical design in power generation and distribution. I am looking to stay within the utility and/or commercial industries and utilize my combined design/engineering experiences to explore new opportunities.

In line with my goal of transitioning towards more engineering in my career I’ve been pursuing a degree in Electrical Engineering with a focus on power systems and distribution. This type of focus falls in line with the experience I already have and the direction I want to go.

Work Experience

My work experience includes layouts of lighting, power, conduit, grounding, equipment and fire detection/notification systems. I utilize the NEC, NFPA, Life Safety Code, and numerous other building codes as well as company and client standards in creating the designs for the required systems.

Additionally, I’ve done power analysis, fault current calculations, and lighting level calculations. These are done in order to determine designs meet requirements and/or to determine what may be needed in order to complete a proper design.

Volunteer Work

I volunteer in bible based education (aiding interested ones in learning from the bible). I find this to be a refreshing and rewarding use of my time. It is helpful to others and continues to strengthen and build my faith as well.

I have also volunteered in building projects. I had many opportunities to help build Kingdome Halls in Michigan. For each project the majority of the work was done in a weekend (meaning starting on Thursday with just a foundation and ending on Sunday with only finishing touches left to be done). These project were amazing. The fellowship and togetherness was uplifting. Even the hard work was fun. This also gave me some valuable experience as I learned how to install ceiling grids and electrical items.

Additionally, I had the opportunity to volunteer for a week in Mississippi after hurricane Katrina where I was part of a team that reinstalled roofs on homes damaged in the storm. Our team managed about 7 roofs that week, a couple were quite large. Before that trip I had never been on a roof, let alone installed one (I’m afraid of open heights!) Although it was sad to see all the destruction and what people were dealing with it was still a wonderful experience in many ways.
